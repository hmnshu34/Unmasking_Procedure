In the ghost of old Cotton Mather, who launched forth fire, brimstone, and all the torments of the damned against the young men who kissed their fiances on Sabbath evenings, could revisit the descendants of his parishioners, read their journals, visit their court rooms and exit sit on the benches of their theatre, what an awful fate would he predict for the degenerate Athenians. It would require more than seven good men to save the tri-mountain village from the fate of the cities of the plain.
