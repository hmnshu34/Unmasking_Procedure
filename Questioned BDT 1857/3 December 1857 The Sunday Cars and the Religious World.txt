3 December 1857
“The Sunday Cars and the Religious World”
We sometime ago took credit to ourselves and the other advocates of Sunday city cars, from the fact that their universally admitted convenience had convinced even the strongest opponents of the utility of the measure. And ever since the cars have been run, so far from rowdyism having been increased or turbulence developed, it has been found that the passengers consist of peaceable and orderly members of society. We make no question that the proportion of religious people who travel there, is quite as large if not larger than in the world at large.
